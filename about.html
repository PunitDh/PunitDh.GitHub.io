About Me

I was born in Mumbai, India. I moved to Singapore at the age of 12 where I completed High School (IB Diploma) then moved overseas for University. I got my electrical engineering degree from UTS in 2013. Eventually I came to realise that electrical engineering was not my field, and I needed to find something that I felt passionately for and cared deeply about.

Why I Became A Developer

My family bought our first computer when I was 8 years old. I was fascinated by it. I recall spending hours trying to figure out every feature of the computer. Eventually at the age of 12, I got into programming. I started with HTML and CSS, then slowly learned JavaScript. I created my first project: a web-based calculator with graphing capabilities at the age of 13. (Unfortunately, this project is now lost to me).

I was also fascinated by video game design. I loved the idea of communicating instructions to the computer that the computer then automates into a process. I loved the idea of automation.

I am fascinated by artificial intelligence and machine learning technologies. In my video game design projects, I attempt to create advanced artificial intelligence in characters using finite state machines (FSMs) and behaviour trees. Growing up, I always had a natural inclination for programming languages. I best expressed my creativity by spending hours designing small projects such as a calculator and a Word Processing tool. Creativity is just one of the many reasons I chose to become a Software Developer.
